BIOGRAPHY

Mahendra Singh Dhoni: Cricket's Captain Cool Born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, India, Mahendra Singh Dhoni, or "MS Dhoni," is a cricketing legend. His journey from a small-town boy to a global icon is a tale of remarkable leadership and skill. Dhoni's explosive batting and agile wicketkeeping earned him a spot on the Indian team in 2004. As captain from 2007, he led India to historic victories in the T20 World Cup (2007) and the Cricket World Cup (2011), ending a 28-year drought. Known as "Captain Cool," Dhoni's calm under pressure, innovative strategies, and batting prowess made him a role model. His legacy as one of cricket's finest finishers endures, inspiring generations. Off the field, Dhoni's humility and sportsmanship left an indelible mark. Despite retiring from Tests in 2014 and limited-overs captaincy in 2017, his influence on cricket remains profound. Mahendra Singh Dhoni's story epitomizes determination, leadership, and unwavering love for the game.

ODI WC 2011

The Cricket World Cup, officially known as ICC Men's Cricket World Cup,[4] is the international championship of One Day International (ODI) cricket. The event is organised by the sport's governing body, the International Cricket Council (ICC), every four years, with preliminary qualification rounds leading up to a finals tournament. The tournament is one of the world's most viewed sporting events and is considered the "flagship event of the international cricket calendar" by the ICC..

ICC Champions Trophy 2013

The ICC Champions Trophy is a One-Day International (ODI) cricket tournament organised by the International Cricket Council. The 2013 tournament was intended to be the final edition of the Champions Trophy, but it was extended to 2017 due to its widespread popularity.
